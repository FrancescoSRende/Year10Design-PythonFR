Megadeth

One of the big four thrash metal bands of the 80s and 90s, Megadeth was arguably the most technically proficient of their peers, often incorporating long, shred-based and neoclassical-influenced solos. A perfect example of this is their fourth album, and arguably their most famous one, the critically acclaimed Rust In Peace. The album boasted the famous and infinitely catchy riffs of Holy Wars... the Punishment Due, their most famous solo from Tornado of Souls and the seemingly endless long solo battles of Hangar 18. But this was merely the culmination of work that had begun three albums prior, with their debut album Killing is My Business...And Business Is Good!. The album presented the same kinds of difficult, technically challenging riffs and solos that would become the norm on Rust In Peace. A perfect example of this is their debut song, one of the strongest album and band openers of all time, Last Rites/Loved to Deth.
