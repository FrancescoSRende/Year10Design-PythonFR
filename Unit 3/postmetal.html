Post-Metal

Post-metal is a genre of metal that incorporates elements of post-rock, post-hardcore, ambient, noise and psychedelia. It takes conventional metal instrumentation and explores texture, atmosphere and emotion. Post-metal songs often lack a conventional verse-chorus structure. Post-metal mainly stems from sludge/doom metal (eg. Melvins, the Ocean, YOB), drone metal (eg. Sunn O))), Earth), industrial metal (eg. Godflesh) and black metal (eg. Deafheaven, Wolves in the Throne Room).

Essential Listening

Godflesh mixed dark industrial and ambient music with lo-fi distorted guitars, crusted basslines and hoarse, grunted or growled vocals to create a terrifying, post-apocalyptic sound.
Deafheaven combined the shrieking vocals, harsh distortion and intense, often blast-beat, drumming of black metal with the fuzzy, ambient, psychedelic sound of shoegaze, and also included melodic, post-rock-esque clean sections.
The Ocean created numerous albums around various topics, such as the evolution of life on earth and the history of heliocentrism. Their songs often feature sludgy guitar riffs with melodic clean passages, and they often experiment with other instruments such as string sections.
